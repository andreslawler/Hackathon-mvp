# Ericsson BCTC Catalogue — Wanted Positions

This catalogue captures Ericsson's wanted position on Sales Business-Critical Terms and Conditions. For each BCTC it summarises the wanted position, any explicitly described fallback, the contractual red line Ericsson must not cross, and a short rationale to support commercial and legal decision-making based strictly on the internal BCTC material.

---

## BCTC-01: Certainty of scope and pricing requirements
- **Wanted position:** Ericsson defines scope clearly and avoids all-inclusive, turnkey, dragnet and continuous improvement obligations at Ericsson's own cost. Fixed price is used only when it is approved in the business case and either scope is final and fixed or Ericsson can adjust price once scope is fully defined.
- **Fallback:** not specified in source
- **Red line:** Do not accept all-inclusive scope, turnkey, dragnet or continuous improvement clauses and do not accept fixed price where scope is open or Ericsson cannot reprice once scope is fixed.
- **Rationale:** Clear scope and controlled pricing avoid unquantified obligations, protect margin and keep delivery risk aligned with the approved business case.

---

## BCTC-02: Product or service availability, roadmap
- **Wanted position:** Ericsson only commits to sell and deliver products or services that already exist and have Product Ready for Tender status. Ericsson does not commit to develop and maintain customer-specific customisations and does not accept liquidated damages or other delay penalties for roadmap delays.
- **Fallback:** not specified in source
- **Red line:** Do not commit to non-PRT or non-existing offerings, customer-specific product development or roadmap-based delay penalties.
- **Rationale:** Limiting commitments to PRT offerings and excluding roadmap penalties keeps development and technology risk under Ericsson's control and protects product strategy and economics.

---

## BCTC-03: Change management, governance
- **Wanted position:** Ericsson keeps a contractual right to approve any change to the agreement or referenced documents and includes a fit-for-purpose change process and governance with escalation to senior executives. For applicable RAN products, the agreement includes a HW supply forecast mechanism with a six-month locked forecast and segmented lead times.
- **Fallback:** not specified in source
- **Red line:** Do not accept change obligations or customer policy updates that bind Ericsson without Ericsson's approval, and do not run significant deliveries without a documented governance and change process.
- **Rationale:** Structured change and governance protect Ericsson from unilateral customer changes, ensure traceability of commercial impact and secure capacity planning for hardware supply.

---

## BCTC-04: Relief from performance
- **Wanted position:** Ericsson must have contractual relief from performance and extensions of time where customer failures, unmet assumptions, or legal restrictions prevent performance. Ericsson is entitled to reimbursement of costs and reasonable price adjustments for customer-caused work and has relief and termination rights where sanctions or export/import denials prevent performance.
- **Fallback:** not specified in source
- **Red line:** Do not accept agreements that hold Ericsson liable without relief when performance is blocked by customer acts, failed assumptions, sanctions or export/import denials.
- **Rationale:** Proper relief provisions ensure Ericsson is not penalised for delays or failures outside its control and allow recovery of cost and restructuring of commitments when circumstances change materially.

---

## BCTC-05: Force Majeure
- **Wanted position:** Ericsson must have Force Majeure relief for circumstances outside its reasonable control, including local conditions and foreseeable external events, with a right to terminate all or part of the agreement after a defined period. Relief extends to events affecting Ericsson's suppliers and subcontractors.
- **Fallback:** not specified in source
- **Red line:** Do not accept Force Majeure clauses that are too narrow, exclude supplier events, or lack a termination right after prolonged Force Majeure.
- **Rationale:** Broad Force Majeure protection preserves Ericsson's ability to stop or exit performance when external events prevent delivery and protects against long-running, loss-making obligations.

---

## BCTC-06: Acceptance
- **Wanted position:** Ericsson includes the prescribed Acceptance Terms, ensures acceptance covers only Ericsson's scope and has clear roles, timelines and re-performance rules. Acceptance is per deliverable for hardware, software and services, without stability periods, and excludes network performance KPIs except for defined network design and optimisation services for radio system sales. Deemed acceptance applies when the customer uses the deliverable or fails to reject within a defined period.
- **Fallback:** Ericsson may agree KPI-based acceptance only for network design and optimisation services for Ericsson radio system sales, not for Cloud RAN, and only with targets limited to agreed KPI-based acceptance that do not delay acceptance or payment.
- **Red line:** Do not accept network performance KPI obligations for products or services generally, acceptance tied to broad network KPIs, stability periods, or the removal of deemed acceptance where use or time triggers are met.
- **Rationale:** Structured acceptance and deemed acceptance mechanisms protect revenue timing, avoid open-ended performance obligations and align technical success criteria with Ericsson's actual scope and control.

---

## BCTC-07: Warranties
- **Wanted position:** Ericsson only provides warranties that align with the Warranty Directive, with standard periods of 12 months from risk transfer for hardware and 3 months from first availability for software.
- **Fallback:** not specified in source
- **Red line:** Do not grant warranty scopes or durations that conflict with the Warranty Directive or extend beyond the defined standard periods without proper approval.
- **Rationale:** Standardised warranties control lifecycle and cost exposure, align with product support models and avoid unbudgeted performance guarantees.

---

## BCTC-08: Right to off-shore and subcontract
- **Wanted position:** Ericsson retains the right to decide service delivery locations and to use and manage subcontractors and suppliers, including cloud providers, subject only to applicable law. Ericsson may accept liability to the customer for subcontractor acts.
- **Fallback:** not specified in source
- **Red line:** Do not accept contractual terms that prevent off-shoring or restrict Ericsson's ability to select and manage subcontractors and suppliers where law permits.
- **Rationale:** Flexibility on delivery location and subcontracting is essential for cost efficiency, resource availability and operational scalability while still allowing Ericsson to front customer-facing liability where appropriate.

---

## BCTC-09: Third party products & services
- **Wanted position:** Where third-party items are significant or critical, Ericsson ensures customer terms are equal to or better than Ericsson's upstream terms and keeps Ericsson's liability proportionate to that of the third party. Ericsson does not offer licenses broader in scope or term than the third party grants to Ericsson.
- **Fallback:** not specified in source
- **Red line:** Do not accept customer terms that create greater liability or broader license rights than Ericsson holds from the third party.
- **Rationale:** Back-to-back alignment with third-party suppliers prevents Ericsson from carrying unhedged pass-through risk or over-committing on rights it does not have.

---

## BCTC-10: Interoperability / compatibility
- **Wanted position:** Ericsson does not unconditionally accept liability for interoperability or compatibility between Ericsson products and third-party or customer components, especially where Ericsson lacks full control or rights.
- **Fallback:** not specified in source
- **Red line:** Do not sign clauses that impose unconditional or broad interoperability liability for mixed environments without securing appropriate rights and controls.
- **Rationale:** Limiting interoperability liability prevents exposure to complex environments and behaviours driven by third-party or customer components beyond Ericsson's control.

---

## BCTC-11: Interface
- **Wanted position:** Ericsson does not provide or license confidential proprietary interface specifications or related parameter settings to customers or their third-party vendors.
- **Fallback:** not specified in source
- **Red line:** Do not agree to disclose Ericsson proprietary interface specifications or parameter settings to customers or their vendors.
- **Rationale:** Protecting proprietary interfaces preserves Ericsson's intellectual property and competitive advantage and reduces interoperability liability risk.

---

## BCTC-12: Assignability / Transfer
- **Wanted position:** Ericsson keeps the right to assign or sell receivables and disclose relevant information for that purpose, while the customer requires Ericsson's prior written consent to assign or transfer the agreement.
- **Fallback:** not specified in source
- **Red line:** Do not waive Ericsson's ability to factor receivables or allow unrestricted customer assignment without Ericsson's consent.
- **Rationale:** Control over assignment preserves financing flexibility for Ericsson and prevents unwanted changes in the contractual counterparty landscape.

---

## BCTC-13: Set-off and late payment interest
- **Wanted position:** The customer has no contractual right of set-off against Ericsson invoices and Ericsson has a contractual right to charge late payment interest.
- **Fallback:** not specified in source
- **Red line:** Do not accept customer set-off rights or waive Ericsson's right to late payment interest.
- **Rationale:** Excluding set-off and charging interest discourage payment delays, protect cash flow and avoid customers unilaterally disputing or netting invoices.

---

## BCTC-14: Insurance requirements
- **Wanted position:** Ericsson does not link limitation of liability to insurance recoveries or accept unlimited liability insurance, does not disclose full policy details or copies, and does not commit to purchase additional or dedicated insurance or insure customer property outside Ericsson's care, custody and control. Customers are not added as insureds or beneficiaries except in tightly defined general/product liability cases and Ericsson avoids unconditional waivers of subrogation.
- **Fallback:** not specified in source
- **Red line:** Do not accept customer demands that expand Ericsson's insurance obligations, expose sensitive insurance information or override standard global insurance programs.
- **Rationale:** Strict insurance positioning prevents unbudgeted insurance costs, protects confidentiality of Ericsson's insurance arrangements and avoids undermining group risk structures.

---

## BCTC-15: Ericsson termination and suspension rights
- **Wanted position:** Ericsson must have termination rights for material breach, license condition breach, confidentiality breach, insolvency, long-running Force Majeure, sanctions or export/import denials and non-payment after cure. For each such circumstance Ericsson must also be able to suspend performance and services at its discretion and must have a termination right for customer change of control.
- **Fallback:** not specified in source
- **Red line:** Do not accept agreements that lack termination and suspension rights for these core triggers, including change of control and regulatory restrictions.
- **Rationale:** Strong termination and suspension rights allow Ericsson to stop performance, manage credit and sanctions risk and exit unsustainable or non-compliant customer relationships.

---

## BCTC-16: Customer termination rights
- **Wanted position:** Customer termination for breach is only allowed for material breach, with written notice and a reasonable cure period. Termination for convenience during a fixed term is only permitted if the customer pays an early termination charge aligned with the approved business case. Term extensions must be mutual and Ericsson charges for exit or transition services in addition to termination charges.
- **Fallback:** not specified in source
- **Red line:** Do not allow free customer termination for convenience in fixed-term agreements or term extensions without Ericsson consent and economically adequate compensation.
- **Rationale:** Controlled termination rights protect committed revenue and investment recovery and ensure that any early exit or transition is properly compensated.

---

## BCTC-17: Limitation of liability
- **Wanted position:** Ericsson's total aggregated liability is capped at 100% of defined fee bases depending on deal type, and the cap covers all liabilities including indemnities, liquidated damages, service credits and penalties. Ericsson may agree mutual uncapped liability only for confidentiality breaches and liability that cannot be limited by mandatory law and excludes personal data from confidentiality where confidentiality liability is uncapped. Ericsson limits indemnity obligations to defined IPR and mutual bodily injury/property damage scopes and ensures any corresponding caps applied to customers do not apply to customer confidentiality breaches, license breaches or non-payment. Ericsson excludes indirect and consequential categories of loss and extends this exclusion to customer claims for corresponding third-party losses.
- **Fallback:** not specified in source
- **Red line:** Do not exceed the defined liability caps without required approvals, do not accept uncapped liabilities beyond those mandated and defined, and do not accept liability for the excluded categories of loss or third-party mirror claims for those losses.
- **Rationale:** Structured caps and exclusions are central to controlling worst-case financial exposure and keeping risk in line with deal value and margin.

---

## BCTC-18: Delays (Liquidated Damages / Service Credits)
- **Wanted position:** Ericsson uses liquidated damages for delay where it commits to delivery times or milestones, with LDs calculated on the value of delayed items, capped at 1% per full week and 10% in total, and with LDs as the sole financial remedy. For recurring services, service credits are used instead of LDs, with clearly defined, controllable KPIs and a monthly cap of 10% of the applicable service fee and service credits as the exclusive financial remedy.
- **Fallback:** As a limited exception, Ericsson may permit the customer to claim additional losses above LDs where the total LD pot is at or below the defined thresholds and only the excess over LDs is recoverable, with no double recovery.
- **Red line:** Do not accept LD or service-credit structures that exceed 20% of the reference amounts or that allow double recovery or additional remedies beyond the capped structures, and do not agree LDs for non-performance topics such as confidentiality.
- **Rationale:** Defined and capped LD and service credit regimes give customers predictable remedies while protecting Ericsson from disproportionate penalty-type exposures and overlapping claims.

---

## BCTC-19: Customer audits
- **Wanted position:** Ericsson only accepts customer audit rights where the customer shows they are needed for compliance with mandatory law and the scope and notice regime are reasonable. Audit rights must not be disruptive, overly frequent or expose unrelated sensitive data, and must follow the separate audit work instruction where applicable.
- **Fallback:** not specified in source
- **Red line:** Do not accept broad, frequent or intrusive audit rights that are not tied to legal obligations or that risk exposing Ericsson's wider operations or third-party data.
- **Rationale:** Tight control of audit rights protects Ericsson's operations, confidentiality and cost base while still allowing legally required oversight.

---

## BCTC-20: No IPR transfer or broad licenses to Ericsson IPR
- **Wanted position:** Ericsson does not sell or transfer title to, or license on an exclusive basis, Ericsson or supplier IPR or know-how as part of standard deliveries. For specific ADM work-for-hire, Ericsson may transfer IPR created under the service only when it is free of Ericsson background IPR, products, tools and know-how and will not be reused for other customers, and licenses to Ericsson IPR are limited strictly to what is needed to use Ericsson deliverables, without extending background IPR to non-Ericsson products or systems or giving non-assert commitments on wider Ericsson IPR.
- **Fallback:** not specified in source
- **Red line:** Do not transfer or exclusively license Ericsson or supplier IPR tied to products or platforms, do not grant broader IPR licenses than needed, and do not agree non-assert or standstill commitments on Ericsson IPR beyond the specific deliverables.
- **Rationale:** Preserving Ericsson's IPR ownership and tight licensing avoids erosion of long-term IP value and protects the broader portfolio beyond a single customer engagement.

---

## BCTC-21: No IPR infringement warranty and IPR indemnity requirements
- **Wanted position:** Ericsson does not warrant non-infringement unless the sole remedy is the defined IPR indemnity. Any IPR indemnity is limited to defending covered third-party claims and paying damages or settlements agreed by Ericsson, with Ericsson controlling the defence and having options to modify, replace or refund. The indemnity excludes claims caused by combinations, unapproved modifications, open source and value attributable to customer data, non-Ericsson products or business, and any indemnity exposure must sit within the general liability cap or equivalent structure and be the customer's exclusive remedy for IPR infringement.
- **Fallback:** Ericsson may accept a non-infringement warranty only where the remedy is strictly limited to the indemnity framework described in this BCTC.
- **Red line:** Do not give open-ended IPR warranties or indemnities, do not accept indemnity scopes or damages bases that go beyond the defined conditions, and do not allow IPR remedies outside the agreed capped indemnity structure.
- **Rationale:** Tight IPR indemnity and warranty terms avoid unlimited exposure to patent and other IPR claims and keep remedy structures consistent with Ericsson's product and licensing models.

---

## BCTC-22: Data access, ownership and use rights
- **Wanted position:** For offerings requiring customer data, Ericsson secures rights to access, store, use, process and transfer the data to perform the agreement and improve and commercialise Ericsson offerings. For certain offerings, Ericsson also secures ownership and commercialisation rights in analytics, learnings, models, insights and similar outputs derived from customer data. All data use complies with mandatory law and access to customer networks uses CAS and/or MSDP as the secure connection method.
- **Fallback:** not specified in source
- **Red line:** Do not accept data terms that block Ericsson's operational use of customer data for delivery or, where required, improvement and commercialisation of offerings, or that prevent mandated secure access patterns.
- **Rationale:** Proper data rights and secure access mechanisms underpin service delivery quality, future product development and compliance with data and security obligations.

---

## BCTC-23: Personal data protection
- **Wanted position:** Agreements that involve personal data include a clear set of data protection obligations as required by law, generally positioning Ericsson as processor or sub-processor and the customer as controller or higher-tier processor. The customer is responsible for having a legal basis and any necessary consents. For restricted cross-border transfers, Ericsson ensures the agreement records customer consent to processing locations and meets applicable transfer requirements.
- **Fallback:** not specified in source
- **Red line:** Do not accept roles or obligations that contradict applicable data protection frameworks or transfer obligations without the required contractual and legal safeguards.
- **Rationale:** Clear allocation of data protection roles and transfer conditions reduces regulatory risk and keeps compliance responsibilities with the party controlling processing purposes.

---

## BCTC-24: Software / Source Code / Escrow
- **Wanted position:** Ericsson only licenses software and never sells it. Ericsson does not provide or license source code or place source code in escrow for the customer's benefit. Licenses are granted to the purchasing customer legal entity only, with no sublicensing to group entities or third parties, while allowing use on the customer's behalf within the license scope. Ericsson secures rights to use or modify customer and other vendors' software needed for Ericsson's services and keeps freedom to update software outside planned upgrades where needed for security or legal reasons.
- **Fallback:** not specified in source
- **Red line:** Do not agree to sell software, provide source code, escrow source code or extend software license scopes beyond the defined customer entity and permitted uses.
- **Rationale:** Retaining software ownership and controlling source code access protects Ericsson's IP, product integrity and security posture.

---

## BCTC-25: Open source
- **Wanted position:** For open source components in Ericsson software, the agreement recognises that open source license terms prevail over the customer agreement, Ericsson controls the format of open source disclosures and the agreement does not require specific formats or disclosures beyond what the open source licenses require. Inclusion or change of open source components is never subject to customer approval.
- **Fallback:** not specified in source
- **Red line:** Do not accept customer approval rights over open source use or contractual obligations that override open source license structures or dictate disclosure formats.
- **Rationale:** Control over open source governance allows Ericsson to comply with license obligations without granting customers control over Ericsson's internal component choices or tooling.

---

## BCTC-26: Most Favoured Customer / Exclusivity / Non-compete
- **Wanted position:** Ericsson does not grant most-favoured customer status or equivalent advantages on price, lead time or obligations, does not accept price benchmarking, does not grant product, feature, solution or service exclusivity and does not accept non-compete commitments restricting work with others or competing offerings.
- **Fallback:** As a limited exception, Ericsson may allow benchmarking for services where the customer has a firm multi-year service commitment, provided there is no automatic adjustment of price or terms based on the benchmark.
- **Red line:** Do not accept most-favoured customer clauses, broad benchmarking with automatic adjustments, exclusivity commitments or non-compete clauses beyond the limited service-benchmarking exception.
- **Rationale:** Rejecting MFC, exclusivity and non-compete restrictions preserves Ericsson's ability to price competitively, serve multiple customers and evolve offerings without contractual constraints tied to a single deal.

---

## BCTC-27: Confidentiality
- **Wanted position:** Ericsson confidential information disclosed to the customer is subject to customer confidentiality obligations and Ericsson keeps rights to share customer confidential information with affiliates, subcontractors and suppliers as needed to perform the agreement. Personal data is carved out from confidential information where Ericsson has agreed unlimited liability for confidentiality breaches.
- **Fallback:** not specified in source
- **Red line:** Do not accept confidentiality regimes that block necessary onward disclosure to Ericsson group entities and delivery partners or that create unbounded liability for personal data under general confidentiality terms.
- **Rationale:** Balanced confidentiality terms protect Ericsson information while enabling operational delivery and align personal data risk with specific data protection frameworks rather than general confidentiality.

---

## BCTC-28: Compliance with laws
- **Wanted position:** The customer remains responsible for complying with its own applicable laws, including health, safety and environmental rules. Where Ericsson is required to comply with anti-bribery and corruption laws, Ericsson's liability and notification duties are limited to the specific engagement, while Ericsson may commit to group-wide policies consistent with such laws without guaranteeing zero violations. The customer warrants that payments are not proceeds of crime and breach of that warranty is a material breach. Contracts require that only the customer pays Ericsson's invoices unless Ericsson consents otherwise. Where items are delivered, the agreement includes mutual export-control compliance obligations, prevents Ericsson from participating in prohibited exports and restricts customer re-exports or transfers without Ericsson consent, supported by rights to terminate or suspend for breach and E-waste management obligations where applicable.
- **Fallback:** not specified in source
- **Red line:** Do not accept compliance clauses that expand Ericsson's anti-corruption or export-control liability beyond the specific engagement or that allow third parties to pay Ericsson without consent or re-export items in breach of export controls or sanctions.
- **Rationale:** Focused compliance obligations and control over payments and export channels limit regulatory and sanctions risk while keeping customers responsible for their own compliance environments.
